Bloch-Feynman vector model for the Stark-induced adiabatic passage process. The Bloch vector R⃗\vec{R}R represents the molecular state, while the field vector F⃗\vec{F}F refers to the combined optical field of the pump and Stokes pulses that drive the Raman transition. In the pseudo space, the Z-component of the Bloch vector R⃗\vec{R}R refers to population inversion between the initial and final vibrational levels. Note that as the Raman detuning Δ\DeltaΔ changes due to the light-induced Stark shift, the ZZZ-component of F⃗\vec{F}F passes through the X-Y plane of the pseudo space and reverses the sign. If R⃗\vec{R}R spins around F⃗\vec{F}F fast enough and F⃗\vec{F}F changes slowly enough, R⃗\vec{R}R will be able to follow F⃗\vec{F}F, eventually also inverting along ZZZ. Inversion of RRR along ZZZ corresponds to inversion of population between the initial and final vibrational levels. This is adiabatic population inversion.
